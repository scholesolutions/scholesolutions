"Scholé" — from the ancient Greek for leisure and learning — reflects our belief that every learner deserves the time and tools to truly flourish.

What we offer

Specialist support services

Tailored, one-on-one programmes using evidence-based, multi-sensory interventions for learners with literacy and numeracy challenges.

📖

Structured Literacy Tutoring

Systematic, phonics-grounded reading and writing support for learners with dyslexia or literacy difficulties, using multi-sensory techniques proven by research.

🔢

Numeracy Support

Targeted maths intervention addressing foundational gaps, building number sense, and developing confidence — aligned with each learner's individual needs.

📋

Dyslexia & Literacy Assessments

Comprehensive dyslexia evaluation and educational achievement assessments in literacy and numeracy, using standardised tools and internationally recognised frameworks.

🎓

SAC Evaluations

Special Assessment Conditions (SAC) evaluations to support students seeking accommodations for NCEA and other formal examinations.

Our approach

Learning through all the senses

True multi-sensory instruction means engaging Visual, Auditory, and Kinesthetic pathways simultaneously — so learning sticks, no matter how the brain is wired.

👁

Visual

Colour coding, visual patterns, and graphic tools help learners see the structure of language and numbers clearly.

👂

Auditory

Phonological awareness, rhythm, and verbal repetition build strong sound-to-symbol connections at every stage.

✋

Kinesthetic

Hands-on activities, movement, and tactile materials make abstract concepts concrete and memorable.

Formal assessments

Comprehensive evaluation services

Standardised assessments delivered by a qualified Level B assessor — giving families, schools, and learners clear answers and a path forward.

Dyslexia Evaluation

In-depth assessment to identify dyslexia and related reading difficulties, including phonological processing, fluency, and comprehension.

SAC Assessment

Special Assessment Conditions evaluations to document learning needs and support applications for exam accommodations in NCEA and tertiary settings.

Educational Achievement

Standardised assessment of literacy and numeracy achievement to identify gaps and inform targeted intervention planning.

Written Report & Recommendations

Every assessment includes a full written report with findings, diagnosis where applicable, and practical recommendations for home and school.

Meet your interventionist

Hi, I'm Eunice.

I'm a dedicated Multi-Sensory Structured Literacy (MSL) interventionist and an Accredited Member of the Australian Dyslexia Association (AMADA), and I am recognised by the International Dyslexia Association (IDA) as a certified dyslexia interventionist.

My journey in literacy specialisation began in 2017 when my daughter was diagnosed with dyslexia. Driven to support her, I pursued structured literacy training through the Institute for Multi-Sensory Structured Language Education (IMSLE) in Australia and achieved MSL reading interventionist accreditation in 2019. By 2023, I had furthered my credentials by earning certification from the International Dyslexia Association Centre for Effective Reading Intervention (CERI).

  • Accredited Member — Australian Dyslexia Association (AMADA)
  • Certified Dyslexia Interventionist — International Dyslexia Association (IDA)
  • MSL Reading Interventionist — IMSLE Australia (2019)
  • IDA Centre for Effective Reading Intervention — CERI (2023)
  • Level B Assessor — Pearson Clinical Australia & NZCER
  • In-person and online sessions available across New Zealand

Working together

Tutoring Agreement

A clear agreement sets the foundation for a strong, trusting learning partnership.

Before sessions begin, every client receives a Tutoring Agreement outlining the responsibilities of both tutor and learner/family. This ensures clarity and trust from the very first session.

The agreement covers:

  • Scheduling and session frequency
  • Communication channels and response times
  • Payment terms and preferred methods
  • Cancellation and rescheduling policy
  • Progress reporting and review periods
