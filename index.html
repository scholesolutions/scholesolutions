Where the Science of Learning Meets the Space to Breathe.

If your child is struggling with reading, writing, or maths, the school day can feel like a constant battleground. When a child is anxious, overwhelmed, or feeling left behind, their brain goes into survival mode— blocking their ability to learn.

At Scholé Solutions, we replace academic panic with evidence-based purpose.

Named after the classical Greek concept of Scholé—meaning restful, unhurried learning—we provide explicit, structured literacy and numeracy intervention in an environment designed to lower stress and build genuine confidence. We don't just teach; we quiet the cognitive anxiety that stands in your child's way, freeing their working memory to map new skills and achieve life-changing, confidence-boosting mastery.

No more guesswork. No more tears. Just explicit, science-backed remediation in a space where your child feels safe to succeed.

Our approach

Learning through all the senses

True multi-sensory instruction means engaging Visual, Auditory, and Kinesthetic pathways simultaneously — so learning sticks, no matter how the brain is wired.

👁

Visual

Explicit visual scaffolds—including directional colour-coding, orthographic grids, and graphic organisers—anchor handwriting mechanics and expose the underlying structure of language and numeracy.

👂

Auditory

Learners build strong phonemic skills by hearing, speaking, and writing simultaneously. Saying letter sounds aloud while practising handwriting strokes provides the immediate feedback needed to lock in sound-to-symbol connections.

✋

Kinesthetic

Guided graphomotor practice and speech-production mechanics provide the vital haptic feedback needed to automate handwriting. This physical execution transforms abstract concepts into tangible, permanent mental models.

About Dr Eunice M. Fuchs

Bridging Clinical Science, Educational Diagnostics, and Compassionate Remediation

I am an experienced tertiary-level educator, learning analytics specialist, and formally trained diagnostician who brings a deeply evidence-informed, multidisciplinary approach to academic intervention. My professional journey bridges cognitive science, healthcare, and inclusive learning environments.

With 24 years of combined experience in clinical nursing and healthcare education, and 7 years of specialised expertise in Structured Literacy and educational diagnostics, I view each learner through a unique, holistic lens. My practice specialises in the early identification of neurodiverse profiles, neurodevelopmental screening, and the management of the additive impact of complex comorbidities on cognitive processing, learning, and well-being.

A Personal Mission, A Professional Standard

My path into literacy specialisation began in 2017 when my daughter was diagnosed with dyslexia. Driven to find the most efficacious, science-backed support for her, I pursued rigorous structured literacy training through the Institute for Multi-Sensory Structured Language Education (IMSLE) in Australia, achieving MSL Reading Interventionist accreditation and becoming an Associate Member of the Australian Dyslexia Association (AMADA) in 2019. Committed to clinical and pedagogical excellence, I earned formal certification from the International Dyslexia Association's Centre for Effective Reading Intervention (CERI) in 2023.

Data-Driven Insights, Inclusive Classrooms

As a Level B Registered Educational Assessor and a Cambridge-Approved Special Access Arrangements Assessor, my practice is deeply grounded in psychometric theory, data analytics, and the principles of the Science of Learning. I translate complex cognitive and achievement data into highly structured, actionable strategies. Whether working one-on-one with a student or helping schools build sustainable internal capabilities for school-wide cognitive profiling, my goal is the same: to remove barriers and implement targeted, high-impact learning interventions that stick.
